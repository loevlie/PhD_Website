What I'm thinking about

A simple mean-pooling MIL baseline matches or outperforms attention-based MIL and 3D-CNN methods on 4 of 6 moderate-sized neuroimage tasks while training 25× faster. My CHIL 2026 benchmark with Ethan Harvey gave me a precise way to see when bag-level pooling is enough — and when correlation between instances genuinely changes the geometry of the problem.

The follow-up: how do we tell, before training, which regime a new dataset is in? Right now we run the full sweep. There should be a cheap diagnostic — something like a per-bag intra/inter variance ratio — that predicts whether attention pays for itself.

Adjacent: tabular foundation models (TabPFN, TabICL). The Frozen Forecaster on the homepage above is my hands-on with in-context learning vs gradient boosting on small synthetic 2D distributions. Next: extend the comparison to real medical-tabular benchmarks where the train sets are actually small.
