What I'm thinking about

A per-instance Gaussian baseline beats transformer-based MIL methods on instance-level metrics in our benchmarks, even though the transformers win on bag-level metrics. That gap matters — instance-level localization is what drives the clinical interpretation of these models. I'm investigating attention-regularization techniques (entropy penalties, sparsity priors, learned-k TopK attention) to drastically improve transformer instance-level metrics without sacrificing bag-level accuracy. Targeting NeurIPS 2026.

On the same axis: our CHIL 2026 benchmark with Ethan Harvey showed a simple mean-pooling MIL baseline matches attention-MIL on 4 of 6 moderate-sized neuroimage tasks while training 25× faster. The follow-up question is how we predict, before training, which regime a new dataset is in.

Looking ahead — picking the central PhD thread on tabular foundation models. Top candidates: V-JEPA-style world models for table dynamics, contrastive table-language alignment ("CLIP for tables") with column-then-row encoders + JEPA-in-modality, scaling-law theory for tabular pretraining, and causal structure inside TFMs. See the /now page for details.
