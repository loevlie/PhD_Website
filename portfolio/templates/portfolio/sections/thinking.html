What I'm thinking about

Our CHIL 2026 benchmark with Ethan Harvey landed two results worth pulling on. First: a simple mean-pooling MIL baseline matches attention-MIL on 4 of 6 moderate-sized neuroimage tasks while training 25× faster. Second — the more interesting one — a centered-Gaussian (Gaussian-axial) baseline beats transformer-based MIL methods on instance-level metrics.

That gap matters for clinical interpretation: if a transformer can't find the right instances but a fixed Gaussian prior on instance positions can, the attention is poorly regularized. I'm investigating attention regularization to close the instance-level gap without hurting bag-level accuracy. Targeting NeurIPS 2026.

Looking ahead — picking the central PhD thread on tabular foundation models. Top candidates: V-JEPA-style world models for table dynamics, contrastive table-language alignment ("CLIP for tables") with column-then-row encoders + JEPA-in-modality, scaling-law theory for tabular pretraining, and causal structure inside TFMs. See the /now page for details.
